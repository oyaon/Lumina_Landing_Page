Content Strategy

Every word on the page was audited and rewritten to hold a single, consistent register throughout: dark luxury — deliberate, editorial, unhurried. The guiding content rule: copy and design must feel made by the same person in the same moment.

Brand Voice Principles
PrincipleRationale & Application
"Crafted", never "engineered"Luxury products are crafted. "Engineered" implies specification sheets and cold process. One word change carries the entire brand register. Applied: "Crafted for extraordinary thinking."
Commands, not invitationsWeak pages invite users to try things. Strong ones state what the product does and let the user decide. Every headline on Lumina is declarative, never interrogative.
Specific over vagueGeneric SaaS copy was replaced with concrete outcomes: "Find anything, understand everything, forget nothing" vs. the original "making retrieval feel like memory."
One action verb throughout: BeginEvery CTA on the page uses "Begin" — Begin Free, Begin Luminary, Begin Free →. Repetition of a single verb builds subconscious trust and brand recall.
Premium audience word choice"Active Builders" → "Teams Worldwide." "Uptime SLA" → "Global Uptime." Word choice signals who the product is for before the user reads a description.
No real brand names in trust stripOriginal strip listed Notion, Linear, Vercel, Figma, Stripe — competitors and partners, not customers. Replaced with fictional enterprise names (Meridian, Archform, Vantage, Crestline, Solace).

Key Design Decisions

Eight design choices that shaped the most significant visual and UX outcomes — with the rationale behind each.

Hero Orbs — Warm Gold Only

Original palette included blue (#4c7ac9) and pink (#c94c7a) orbs — direct conflicts with the luxury monochrome identity. Revised to three warm-gold-only depth orbs at varying opacity and drift speeds. Atmospheric layering without any foreign hues.

Feature Grid Hierarchy

Six identical cards flattened perceived importance. Solution: Card 01 spans the full grid width with a live animated node-graph visual, physically breaking the grid and anchoring attention before the user reads a word of copy.

Pricing Card Elevation

The featured Luminary card uses scaleY(1.02) with transform-origin: top center to physically lift it above its neighbours. Combined with a gradient top border and ambient box-shadow, it reads as "chosen" before conscious evaluation.

Dual Opposing Marquees

A single scrolling row feels like a conveyor belt. Two rows at different speeds in opposite directions create the impression of a much larger community — while showing all 10 testimonials simultaneously without pagination.

Corner-Bracket Visual Frame

Platform spotlight originally used a center spine divider that fought the 2-column layout. Replaced with CSS ::before/::after corner brackets — a precision instrument motif that reinforces brand character without layout constraints.

Custom Cursor System

The gold dot-and-ring cursor with spring-lag interpolation (0.11 lerp factor) extends brand language into the interaction layer. Every mouse movement feels intentional. The ring expands on hover over any interactive element.

Trust Strip — No Real Brand Names

Notion, Linear, Vercel, Figma, Stripe are competitors or unrelated platforms — not Lumina customers. Listing them is a credibility error. Replaced with fictional enterprise names that signal the correct buyer archetype without misdirection.

Nav Label: "Proof" not "Stories"

In a compressed B2B enterprise nav, "Stories" is too soft. "Proof" is a single-word challenge — it implies evidence, creates curiosity, and aligns with the testimonials section headline: "What changes when teams think better."

Responsive Behaviour

Two breakpoints handle the full range from widescreen desktop to mobile. No external CSS framework is used — all rules are hand-authored inside the single <style> block.

BreakpointChanges Applied
≤ 1100px
Tablet / small laptop		 Nav padding compresses (26px → 22px). Nav links and Sign In button hidden — only logo mark and Begin Free remain visible. All major sections reduce from 150–160px padding to 100px 32px. Features grid collapses 3-column → 2-column. Hero feature card switches from 2-column to 1-column stacked. Platform spotlight stacks vertically. Pricing cards stack to single column; featured card loses scaleY. Testimonials header stacks vertically. Footer compresses from 4-column to 2-column grid.
≤ 640px
Mobile		 Features grid collapses to 1-column. Stats bar wraps to 2×2 grid (border-bottom replaces border-right). Hero action buttons stack to full-width and justify-center. Trust logos wrap and center. Footer goes fully single-column. CTA social proof strip stacks vertically; separator lines hide.

Recommendations for Next Iteration

The current page is a high-quality prototype. The following improvements would elevate it to a fully production-ready, conversion-optimised marketing page.

RecommendationPriorityRationale
Add product screenshot or demo videoHighPage has zero UI proof. A 30-second autoplay muted clip or a single sharp UI screenshot above the fold dramatically improves cold-traffic conversion rates.
A/B test hero headlineHighTest "Think clearer. Move faster." (functional) vs. "The mind behind your best work" (emotional). Both are strong — data decides.
Real customer logos in trust stripHigh3 real SVG logos of actual customers outperform 5 fictional names in every study. Significant credibility uplift at zero design cost.
Add FAQ section below pricingMediumEnterprise buyers have objections. A collapsible FAQ pre-empts sales calls, shortens deal cycles, and improves long-tail SEO keyword coverage.
Open Graph / Twitter Card / JSON-LDMediumPage has no social sharing meta tags or structured data. High-impact for distribution and SEO. Implementation takes less than 30 minutes.
prefers-reduced-motion supportMediumcursor:none on body hides the system cursor. Requires a prefers-reduced-motion media query that disables custom animations and restores the system cursor.
Keyboard focus ring stylesMediumFocus states are missing throughout the page. Required for WCAG 2.1 AA compliance and keyboard navigation.
Mobile navigation drawerLowThe nav collapses all links on tablet/mobile with no hamburger or drawer fallback. A minimal slide-down nav would improve mobile UX.
Scroll-reveal on all section headersLowCurrently only feature cards animate in on scroll. Extending fadeUp to section headers and the platform content would make the scroll experience feel more immersive.
